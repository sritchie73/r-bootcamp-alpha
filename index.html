General Information

Software Carpentry's mission is to help scientists and engineers become more productive by teaching them basic lab skills for computing like program design, version control, data management, and task automation. This two-day hands-on bootcamp will cover basic concepts and tools; participants will be encouraged to help one another and to apply what they have learned to their own research problems.

Schedule

The bootcamp will run from 9-4:30, and will be split into four sessions: there will be two 15 minute coffe breaks at 10:30 and 14:30, and a one hour lunch break at 1pm.

Tea and coffee will be provided, and microwaves are available.

Syllabus

The syllabus will teach the fundamentals of scientific programming, covering the foundations of software development and design, via an introduction to the R statistical programming language.

The high level concepts this bootcamp subset will impart are defensive programming, error handling, debugging, unit testing and test-driven development. These skills are transferrable to any language.

The bootcamp also seeks to cover the fundamentals of the R language, providing a solid foundation to learn from. R materials will be selected from the Software Carpentry intermediate R material Ideally bootcamp attendees will have used R before, and want to learn more, however complete novices are also welcome.
